What is the Book of Abraham and how did we get it?

Where did the book come from?

In 1835, a man named Michael Chandler visited Kirtland with 4 mummies and a handful of Egyptian papyri.

What was on the scrolls?

Most sources agree the Papyri contained ancient Egyptian heiroglyphics and pictures. In his journal, Joseph Smith claims he "...found that one of the rolls contained the writings of Abraham.‚Äù



How did Joseph translate the records?

Some sources claim Joseph spent a great deal of time learning and analyzing Egyptian characters. Other historians believe Joseph translated the papyri in a similar fassion to how he "translated" the Bible.



How did Joseph "translate" the Bible?

Many members of the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-Day Saints say Joseph translated the Bible. However, that depends on your definition of translation. In Joseph's time, translation could have been referred to as "to interpret," or "to explain." Joseph's translation of the Bible can be likened to a home renovation: some parts of the bible were taken out, some left original, and some improved upon.



Where are the Papyri now?

Joseph's family sold the papyri and mummies in 1856. Historians generally believe that the mummies and most of the papyri were destroyed in the Great Chicago Fire in 1871.



Do modern translations of the remaining fragments agree with Joseph Smith's translation?

Many Egyptologists do not agree with Joseph's interpretations of the facsimilies contained in the Book of Abraham. The surviving papyri have been identified as part of a funerary scroll. None of the surviving text matches anything in the Book of Abraham.



Was Joseph Smith lying about the papyri?

That is up for debate. A critical viewpoint can assume that Joseph made up his translation from a funeral scroll. A believing opinion can theorize that the surviving papyri were not part of the translated text Joseph published. Neither opinion can be completely proven.



How can I learn for myself?

Latter-Day Saint's believe the Lord revealed that all should "...seek ye diligently and teach one another words of wisdom; yea, seek ye out of the best books words of wisdom, seek learning even by study and also by faith." (Doctrine and covenants 88:118) The best sources to find reliable information include the Joseph Smith Papers Website and Gospel Topics Essays.



Thinking Slowly

To learn the truth about complex subjects like the Book of Abraham, a someone must learn to think slowly. Thinking slowly involves separating the facts from interpretations, and removing assumptions from our thinking. For example, an assumed solution to the question, "Did Joseph Smith lie about his translation?" could be, "Yes, he lied because modern translations of the suriving papyri differ from what he published."



Interpretation vs Fact

To find the truth in the matter, interpretations need to be removed from their associated facts. For example, some Latter-Day Saints would interpret the funeral scroll to be a part of the papyri Joseph didn't translate. Unfortunately, the only fact of the matter is that the scroll exists, and much of it was destroyed. We do not know if Joseph used that particular text in translation.



Conclusion

While the exact details of the translation process remain unknown, both beleivers and non-believers need to draw a conclusion about the authenticity of the Book of Abraham. Latter-Day Saints believe that someone can learn of its authenticity by studying the text and by thinking slowly about what it means.
Personally, I believe Joseph translated the Book of Abraham, though I do not know exactly how. I believe that anayone can learn for themselves through study and faith.
